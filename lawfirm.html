    Time Matters

    In Pennsylvania, the statute of limitations for negligence cases is two years. This means that victims have a two-year window with which to file a lawsuit in civil court against an alleged negligent party. Failure to file a lawsuit in this amount of time could result in an injury victim being unable to recover any compensation for their losses.

    With this in mind, the personal injury lawyers at the Pittsburgh office of Shenderovich, Shenderovich & Fishman want to encourage you to speak to an attorney as soon as possible about your case. It is never a good idea to wait to file a personal injury claim. As soon as an accident occurs, evidence begins to disappear. Over time, memories can fade. The longer you wait to file your personal injury lawsuit, the less likely you are to recover maximum compensation for your expenses.
